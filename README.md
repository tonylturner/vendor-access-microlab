# Find the Persistent Paths

A 30-minute tabletop micro-lab for ICS/OT security practitioners. You will look at a fictional electric distribution utility three weeks after a substation emergency, identify how vendor remote-access paths quietly become permanent, and confront one of the most underdiscussed problems in operational technology: vendor access is not temporary.

You will need a phone or laptop with a camera and a web browser. No app downloads. No accounts. No advance preparation.

---

## What you will do

The lab runs in three phases over 30 minutes. Your instructor drives pacing from the projector. You work on your own device.

**Phase 1: Classify (about 7 minutes).** You see a network map on the projector. You open a worksheet on your phone. You tap a sticker on each vendor access path you find, fill in how long it has been active, and hunt for six hidden risks embedded on the map.

**Phase 2: Kill three (about 7 minutes).** A simulated incident occurs. You pick three access paths to disable, working under an operational budget. You vote on the projector poll. The room's collective choices appear in real time.

**Phase 3: Time leap (about 10 minutes).** Six months pass in the scenario. No incident occurs. You look at what you did and did not remove. You vote one more time. You see what the room thinks about its own environment.

---

## What you need

Bring a phone or laptop with a working camera and a web browser. That is all.

During the lab, your instructor will project two QR codes:

1. One scans to the **worksheet**, a single web page where you work through all three phases.
2. One scans to the **live polls**, run in Mentimeter or Slido, where you vote during the lab.

## About the worksheet

The worksheet saves your work automatically to your browser. If your phone goes to sleep, your tab closes, or you accidentally refresh, your progress is restored when you come back. A small "saved" indicator flashes briefly after each action.

If you want to start over at any point, the "Reset" button in the top-right header clears everything.

The worksheet has three phases that match the lab's three phases. Phase 1 is open immediately. Phases 2 and 3 nominally wait for the instructor's cue, but you can preview them if you want. The lab works best in sequence, but nothing stops you.

---

## What you will not need

* You will not need to type long answers. The worksheet uses tap-to-select controls almost everywhere.
* You will not need an account on any platform.
* You will not need to download an app. The worksheet and the polls both run in your browser.
* You will not need to install a QR code scanner. Modern phone cameras handle QR codes natively.

---

## After the lab

If you want to revisit the worksheet, your browser will still have your final state saved. You can reopen the URL any time and see what you did. The lab's quantitative results, meaning what the room voted in aggregate, will be shared by your instructor.
